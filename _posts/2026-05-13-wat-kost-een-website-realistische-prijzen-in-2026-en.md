What does a website cost? It's the question I get asked most often. And it's also the hardest to answer briefly, because it's a bit like asking "what does a car cost?" — a used Fiat is not the same as a new Tesla.

In this article, I'll give you clear price ranges so you know what to expect. No hidden rates, just honest numbers.

## What determines the price?

The cost of a website depends on five factors:

1. **Complexity** — How many pages? What features?
2. **Design** — Custom design or template?
3. **CMS** — WordPress, TYPO3, Webflow or custom?
4. **Content** — Who provides the copy and images?
5. **Who builds it** — Freelancer, agency, or DIY?

## Price ranges per website type

### Simple information site (5-10 pages)
Think: a one-pager for a freelancer, a brochure site for a local business.

* WordPress with template: €1,000 - €3,000
* Webflow (self-managed): €1,500 - €3,500
* Custom HTML/CSS: €2,000 - €4,000

### Medium business site (10-25 pages)
Think: an SME with multiple services, portfolio, news section.

* WordPress: €3,000 - €7,000
* TYPO3: €5,000 - €12,000
* Webflow: €3,500 - €8,000

### Advanced custom site (25+ pages)
Think: product catalog, multilingual, webshop, member section.

* TYPO3: €10,000 - €25,000
* WordPress + WooCommerce: €7,000 - €18,000
* Custom (Laravel): €15,000 - €40,000

## What's NOT included?

| Item | Cost |
|-----|------|
| Copywriting | €500 - €2,000 |
| Professional photos | €300 - €1,500 |
| Logo / branding | €500 - €2,500 |
| Hosting (per year) | €100 - €600 |
| Domain (per year) | €10 - €50 |
| Maintenance (per month) | €50 - €200 |

## Conclusion

A good website costs between €2,000 and €15,000, depending on what you need. On top of that come annual hosting and maintenance costs.

Always ask for a clear quote with specifications, and be wary of offers that seem too good to be true.

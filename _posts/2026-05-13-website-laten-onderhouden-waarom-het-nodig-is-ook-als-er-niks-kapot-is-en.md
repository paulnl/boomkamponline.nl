My website works fine, doesn't it? I hear this regularly when I call clients about maintenance. The answer is: yes, for now. But a website isn't a printed page — it's a technical product that needs attention.

This article explains why regular maintenance is essential, what happens when you skip it, and what it costs.

## Why does a website need maintenance?

A website consists of multiple layers that all need updating:

* The CMS (WordPress, TYPO3, etc.) — security updates
* Plugins and extensions — compatibility and security
* The server — PHP, database, SSL certificate
* Backups — in case something goes wrong
* Content — outdated information, broken links

Neglect any of these layers, and your site becomes slower, less secure, and eventually unusable.

## What happens without maintenance?

### Phase 1: After 3-6 months
You miss a few security updates. Most likely, nothing happens. But your site slowly becomes a target.

### Phase 2: After 6-12 months
A plugin stops working after a CMS update. A form breaks, or the layout is broken. Visitors can't contact you. You're losing leads without knowing it.

### Phase 3: After 12-24 months
Your site gets hacked or taken down. This happens more often than you think — millions of WordPress sites are attacked every year. Without a backup, you lose everything.

### Phase 4: After 24+ months
The CMS is so outdated that an update is no longer possible without rebuilding the entire site. The costs are higher than if you had done yearly maintenance.

## What does a good maintenance plan include?

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| CMS updates | Monthly |
| Plugin/extension updates | Monthly |
| Backups | Weekly |
| Test backups (can I restore?) | Monthly |
| Check SSL certificate | Monthly |
| Speed test | Quarterly |
| Check broken links | Quarterly |
| Test contact forms | Monthly |
| Google Analytics check | Monthly |
| Major CMS version upgrade | Yearly |

## How much does maintenance cost?Basic maintenance (WordPress, few changes): €30 - €60/month

Advanced maintenance (TYPO3, custom, regular changes): €75 - €150/month

Incidental support (per hour): €50 - €100/hour

## Can you do it yourself?

Yes, if you're technically inclined. But be honest: will you actually update every month? Will you make backups? Will you test if they work? Many business owners keep meaning to get around to it.

A maintenance contract isn't expensive compared to the cost of a hacked site, a new site because the old one is outdated, or lost revenue because your contact form wasn't working.
